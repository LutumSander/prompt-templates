Research buyer personas for [YOUR COMPANY DOMAIN] - the people who make buying decisions.

Focus on 2-3 key roles. For EACH role:

JOB TITLES:
- Primary job title
- Alternative titles or variations
- Level/seniority (manager, director, VP, C-suite)
- Department (sales, marketing, ops, IT, etc.)

ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
- What they're responsible for
- Day-to-day activities
- Teams they manage (if any)
- Who they report to

BACKGROUNDS:
- Typical career path to this role
- Years of experience (typical)
- Educational background
- Previous roles
- Skills or expertise

WHAT THEY CARE ABOUT:
- Goals and objectives (personal and professional)
- How they're measured (KPIs, OKRs)
- What success looks like for them
- Challenges or pressures they face
- Priorities

PAIN POINTS:
- Top 3-5 problems they face
- Why these problems matter
- Current approaches to solving them
- Frustrations with status quo
- In their exact words (from reviews, forums, LinkedIn)

DECISION-MAKING:
- How they evaluate solutions
- What criteria matter most
- Who else is involved in decisions
- Budget authority level
- Risk tolerance

HOW TO ENGAGE:
- What gets their attention
- Preferred communication channels
- Messaging that resonates
- Proof they need to see
- Common objections

REAL EXAMPLES:
- 15-20 quotes from people in this role about:
  - Their problems
  - What they value
  - Their decision process
- LinkedIn posts showing what they talk about
- Job descriptions for this role

Sources: G2 reviews (filter by role), LinkedIn profiles and posts,
job descriptions, industry forums, Reddit, sales call transcripts

OUTPUT FORMAT
Always provide the output as an artifact in markdown format (.md). 
No explanation or implementation timelines. 
Just the output as an artifact based on the prompt.
